हिरण्यवर्णांहरिणींसुवर्णरजतस्रजाम्।
   चन्द्रांहिरण्मयींलक्ष्मींजातवेदोमआवह।।

Sandhi Breakdown:

Source:

Śr īS ūktam , Rigvedic Hymn, traditionally part of the Khila Suktas  of the Rigveda.

English Translation:

"Bring to me the golden-hued one, radiant and auspicious, adorned with garlands of gold and silver; the luminous, resplendent, and benevolent Goddess Lakshmi, O J ātaveda (Fire God)."

Detailed Grammatical Analysis:

  1. Case Analysis :
  • Many words are in the accusative case , indicating they are the objects of the verb आवह(āvaha)  (bring forth). Examples: हिरण्यवर्णाम्, हरिणीम्, लक्ष्मीम्.
  • जातवेदः  is in the vocative case , as the speaker addresses the Fire God directly.
  • म(ma)  is in the accusative case , as the request is "to me."
  1. Verb Analysis :
  • The verb आवह(āvaha)  is in the imperative mood , second person singular, expressing a command or request.
  1. Compound Analysis :
  • हिरण्यवर्णाम् : Karmadh āraya  compound meaning "golden-hued."
  • सुवर्णरजतस्रजाम् : Bahuvr īhi  compound describing "one whose garlands are of gold and silver."
  • जातवेदः : Tatpuru ṣa  compound meaning "knower of all beings."
  1. Sandhi Observations :
  • जातवेदःम→जातवेदोम : The visarga ः  changes to ओ  before म , following sandhi rules for smoother recitation.
  1. Vedic vs. Classical Sanskrit Usage of "म(Ma)":

      The word म(ma) in the phrase जातवेदोमआवहis consistent with Vedic Sanskrit and is not exclusive to Classical Sanskrit.

    In Vedic Sanskrit, म(ma) is frequently used as the accusative form of the pronoun अहम्(aham), meaning "me "or "to me."

    The Vedic hymns often address deities with म(ma) to express personal devotion or to seek blessings, making its use in this verse entirely appropriate and not derived from Classical Sanskrit.

Why No Sandhi Here?

  1. Phonetic Clarity in Vedic Sanskrit :
  • In Vedic Sanskrit , the phrase मआवह  is intentionally left disjointed. The separation emphasizes the meaning of म(me)  as distinct from आवह(bring forth) .
  • Combining it into माआवह  could potentially lead to confusion with माऽवह , where मा  (with a long vowel) functions as a prohibitive particle, meaning "do not."
  1. Vedic Chanting Rules :
  • In Vedic recitations, preserving clarity is paramount, especially in requests to the divine. Here, separating म  from आवह  ensures the listener (human or divine) unmistakably understands the phrase as "Bring to me " , rather than "Do not bring."
  1. Intended Grammatical Role :
  • म(ma)  is in the accusative case  (direct object), while आवह  is the imperative verb. The separation ensures the grammatical relationship remains unambiguous.
  1. Comparison to Classical Sanskrit :
  • In Classical Sanskrit , more extensive sandhi rules might allow phrases like मआवह  to merge if context permits. However, in Vedic Sanskrit, functional clarity often takes precedence over phonetic elision.

Cultural and Philosophical Importance of Separation:

  • The clear articulation of मआवह  reflects the humility and precision  of Vedic prayers, where every syllable is carefully pronounced to convey the devotee 's request accurately.


Phonetic Features:

  • Vowel Lengths : Long vowels like आं  add a sense of auspiciousness and rhythm.
  • Sandhi Transitions : Smooth connections between words like जातवेदःम→जातवेदोम  ensure melodic chanting.

Cultural Significance:

  1. Lakshmi 's Symbolism :
  • Goddess Lakshmi represents wealth, prosperity, and auspiciousness .
  • Her golden hue and radiant garlands symbolize abundance and divine beauty.
  1. Invocation of Agni (Fire God) :
  • Agni (J ātaveda) is invoked as the intermediary who carries offerings to the deities, bridging the human and divine realms.
  • This reflects Vedic rituals where fire is central to yaj ñas (sacrificial offerings).
  1. Garlands of Gold and Silver :
  • The gold and silver garlands symbolize material and spiritual wealth, showing the dual nature of prosperity in the material and divine realms.

Philosophical Significance:

  1. Connection of Divine and Devotee :
  • The verse emphasizes humility, as the devotee requests the divine through J ātaveda 's assistance.
  • "Bring to me "  reflects a longing for inner abundance, not just material wealth.
  1. Wealth as a Tool for Dharma :
  • Lakshmi ’s blessings are sought not for greed but for fulfilling dharma  (righteous duties), showing the spiritual perspective on material prosperity.
  1. Unity of Material and Spiritual :
  • The description of Lakshmi blends material beauty (gold, silver) with spiritual radiance (resplendent and luminous), symbolizing that true wealth is holistic.
